# Net Opposing Postings Within a Transaction

For capacity and directional Resolution Limits, Ledger derives one Net Account Effect by summing all Postings against the same Account within one indivisible Transaction. Posting amounts are strictly positive and Debit or Credit carries direction; zero or negative amounts are malformed input. A positive effect consumes Increase Capacity, a negative effect consumes Decrease Capacity, and zero consumes neither. An individual Account effect may be zero in an otherwise non-zero Transaction, preserving gross pass-through, fee, or sweep legs. At least one effect must be non-zero; otherwise the valid proposal receives the stable No Economic Effect rejection and no Transaction or Posting is accepted. The original Postings of an accepted Transaction remain immutable for audit.

Net Account Effects from distinct Transactions never offset, including linked Posted and Continuation Transactions created by one Transaction Resolution. They are aggregated gross by direction because their lifecycles can diverge; any resolution movement outside the original directional limits requires current Restrictions and a fresh Availability Check.
